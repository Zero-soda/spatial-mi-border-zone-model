# Statistics and Reproducibility

No statistical method was used to predetermine sample size. Discovery used all six selected GSE214611 MI sections; external mouse transfer used nine GSE176092 MI sections and four GSE265828 sections. The prespecified human panel used nine independent Kuppe patients, with three control, three border-containing and three fibrotic-zone sections. Spot-level maps are treated as spatially correlated visualizations and sensitivity analyses, not as independent biological replicates.

The primary quantitative unit is the biological sample or the stage-domain summary derived from sample-level values. Directional conclusions were evaluated using leave-one-sample-out analysis, single-module dropout analysis, replicate-level domain 3/4 boundary-direction checks, sample-level effect sizes, spatial autocorrelation, domain-label permutation, spatial-block permutation, signed boundary-distance gradients, alternative scoring, expression-matched random-signature controls, Visium graph-boundary analysis, score-only and graph-smoothed state clustering, cell-type marker-adjusted contrast audits and human expression-matched spatial-null tests. External transfer used rank-biserial effects, leave-one-biological-unit-out direction consistency and simple prespecified baselines. Therapeutic-prioritization sensitivity additionally used six leave-one-section-out runs, component omission, plus-or-minus 25% component weights, matched spatial-score nulls and pharmacology-source concordance. Public-data-only robustness preserved 28/28 leave-one-sample-out checks, 15/15 module-dropout checks, 4/4 boundary-direction checks and 10/10 stage-threshold sensitivity checks. The two mechanical-border checks and the day 7 fibroblast-scar repair check exceeded the 99th percentile of equal-size random-signature controls. In expression-matched random-signature controls, both mechanical-border checks exceeded the 99th percentile, while the day 7 fibroblast-scar and immune-fibrotic checks exceeded the 95th percentile.

Randomization and blinding were not applicable to this secondary analysis of publicly available transcriptomic data. The source-data package maps every plotted result to the processed tables used to generate the corresponding figure.
