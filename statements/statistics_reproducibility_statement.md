# Statistics and Reproducibility

No statistical method was used to predetermine sample size. The mouse discovery and temporal validation analyses included all six selected public GSE214611 MI Visium samples available for the prespecified day 3 and day 7 comparison. The human transfer analysis used the public GSM6613090 human STEMI Visium sample. Spot-level maps are treated as spatially correlated visualizations and sensitivity analyses, not as independent biological replicates.

The primary quantitative unit is the biological sample or the stage-domain summary derived from sample-level values. Directional conclusions were evaluated using leave-one-sample-out analysis, single-module dropout analysis and replicate-level domain 3/4 boundary-direction checks. Public-data-only robustness preserved 28/28 leave-one-sample-out checks, 15/15 module-dropout checks, 4/4 boundary-direction checks and 10/10 stage-threshold sensitivity checks. The two mechanical-border checks and the day 7 fibroblast-scar repair check exceeded the 99th percentile of equal-size random-signature controls.

Randomization and blinding were not applicable to this secondary analysis of publicly available transcriptomic data. The source-data package maps every plotted result to the processed tables used to generate the corresponding figure.
